When structuring a project, I focus on decomposition through data abstraction. The goal is to manage complexity. Break the system into independent modules, each hiding its internal implementation behind a clean, stable interface. This ensures you can reason about, modify, or even replace one part of the system without causing a cascade of failures elsewhere.

Each station is a self-contained expert, responsible for its own tasks. This modularity is the key to building robust, maintainable systems that can evolve gracefully over time.
