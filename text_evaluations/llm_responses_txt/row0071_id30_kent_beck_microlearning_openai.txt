As Kent Beck emphasizes in Extreme Programming, continuous integration means merging and testing tiny changes many times a day. It keeps the codebase always buildable, shrinks the gap between cause and effect, and turns big surprises into quick corrections. Small merges stay small; defects appear where they begin; the team shares one truth.
